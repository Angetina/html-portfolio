About Me

Hello, my name is Emily Chiang. I have over four years of experience working as a research assistant in molecular and cellular biology, where I focused on data analysis and automating experiment processes. During this time, I realized the challenges of managing data in biomedical research. To improve efficiency, I taught myself Python and developed scripts to automate data processing tasks.

Last year, I’m studying computer science at the University of the People to strengthen my technical background. I’ve completed several web development projects, such as a task management tool and a countdown timer application. My goal is to learn more about data systems and full-stack development through Northeastern University’s Information Systems program. I want to apply these skills to improve data management and analysis in biomedical research.

I’m passionate about data engineering and software development, and my long-term goal is to become a consultant in the bio-development industry. I hope to help companies in Taiwan create software solutions that speed up research and development.
